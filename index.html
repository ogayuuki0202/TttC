Supersonic Flow Control and Interaction Studies (85 items，8.0%) slope=0.48%/yr

The participants' discussions revolved around advanced fluid dynamics and shock wave studies. Key topics included the effects of particle temperature on drag coefficient, shock wave vibration in direct shock wave/boundary layer interaction, and the impact of flow-interference techniques on hypersonic jets. There was also significant interest in the study of shock wave interactions in various scenarios, such as between two bodies in supersonic flow or around a flexible body in supersonic airflow. Other notable discussions focused on the numerical analysis of critical nozzles, the formulation of high-pressure equations, and the study of particle motion in high-speed gas-solid two-phase flow. The use of unsteady jets for shock wave generation and the interaction of supersonic vehicle wakes with oblique shock waves were also explored.

Laser-Induced Shock Waves and High-Speed Observational Techniques (81 items，7.6%) slope=0.06%/yr

The participants' discussions revolved around advanced research in shock wave phenomena and related technologies. Key topics included the use of phase shift interferometers, birefringent crystals, and femtosecond lasers for studying and measuring shock waves. High-speed photography and X-ray imaging were also highlighted as crucial tools for observing ultra-high-speed events and unsteady forces. The role of optics in three-dimensional reconstruction of shock wave development was also discussed. Additionally, the study of pressure characteristics, electron temperature, and number density in shock waves were considered important. The use of point diffraction interferometry for visualizing low Reynolds flow and detonation wave fine structure was also a significant point of discussion.

Shock Wave Interactions and Propagation in Various Media (81 items，7.6%) slope=0.39%/yr

The participants discussed a wide range of topics related to shock wave research, including the generation of electron plasma waves in ionospheric plasma, shock wave modulation, and the impact of shock wave-object interaction. They also explored the relationship between impact propagation velocity and material properties in powders, and the future prospects of research into non-equilibrium thermochemical processes induced by strong shock waves. Other topics included shock wave mitigation, diffraction, and the effects of shock waves on various media. The discussions also touched on the use of flame-retardant nonwoven fabric for shock wave interference, and the impact of various factors on cavitation due to sudden acceleration.

Shock Wave Analysis and Visualization in Tubes (69 items，6.5%) slope=-0.46%/yr

The participants' discussions revolved around the investigation and analysis of shock waves in various contexts. Key topics included the interaction of shock waves with different structures and materials, the impact of shock waves on gas mixtures, and the use of numerical simulations to study these phenomena. The role of shock tubes in generating high-temperature, low-density fields and visualizing shock wave interactions was also highlighted. Additionally, the influence of particle size on dust accumulation stirred up by shock waves was examined. The discussions also touched on the vibrational relaxation of polyatomic molecules behind shock waves and the characteristics of excess pressure behind shock waves propagating through temperature fluctuation fields.

Free-Flight and Reentry Aerodynamics Testing (65 items，6.1%) slope=-0.31%/yr

The participants discussed a range of topics related to space exploration and technology. Key points included the use of a Hayabusa capsule model for various tests and measurements, the development of a deployable flexible aeroshell, and the control of flow fields behind re-entry vehicles. They also discussed the development of a space debris collection device, the launch of the CARS system, and the use of various experimental and simulation methods to understand and improve the re-entry environment of sample return capsules. The impact of surface catalysis on communication blackouts during missions was also a topic of discussion.

Underwater Shock Waves and Cavitation Studies (65 items，6.1%) slope=-0.31%/yr

The participants discussed a range of topics related to underwater pressure, shock waves, and microbubbles. They explored the effects of these phenomena on various materials, including metal plates, resin, and textiles. Key areas of interest included the control of microbubble motion, the impact of shock waves on material structures, and the use of shock waves in processes such as resin separation and azo dye decomposition. The discussions also touched on the practical applications of these studies, such as in concrete destruction and micro-deburring of implants.

Rotating Detonation Engine Research and Development (63 items，5.9%) slope=0.34%/yr

The participants' discussions revolved around the research, development, and testing of various aspects of detonation engines, particularly rotating detonation engines (RDEs). Key topics included the impact of different components (like swirl injectors, exhaust nozzles, and diverging channels) on engine performance, the use of different fuels (like ethanol and hydrogen-oxygen), and the visualization of internal phenomena. Other areas of interest included the influence of combustion chamber flowpath, the effect of operating frequency on spraying performance, and the potential for thrust vector control. The discussions also touched on more specific areas such as the use of a resonance tube ignition device, the enhancement of turbulent heat transfer on teardrop dimpled walls, and the study of a self-actuating system in a rotary detonation turbine engine.

Hypersonic Flow Analysis and Control Methods (61 items，5.7%) slope=0.33%/yr

The participants' discussions revolved around the numerical and experimental analysis of hypersonic and supersonic aerodynamics. Key topics included the aerodynamic characteristics of multi-role aircraft at various speeds, the use of hypersonic wind tunnels for research and education, and the simulation of high hypersonic heat flux. There was also a focus on the numerical evaluation of various configurations and the influence of pressure waveforms on turbulent interaction effects. The use of open-source CFD software for simulations was highlighted, along with the challenges of real gas modeling in hypersonic flight environments. The discussions also touched on international standardization of high-speed crash test procedures and hypervelocity impact experiments.

Blast Wave Mitigation and Safety Assessment (59 items，5.6%) slope=0.21%/yr

They also highlighted the need for technological advancements in blast wave attenuation and the development of blast simulators. Additionally, the role of numerical simulations in studying unexploded underwater explosives and the impact of explosives on structures was underscored. The discussion also touched on the use of protective gear like helmets in blast attenuation and the initiatives of the National Police Agency in countering raw materials for explosives.

Impact Damage and Crack Propagation in Various Materials (57 items，5.4%) slope=-0.44%/yr

The participants' discussions revolved around the impact damage and structural changes in various materials due to high-speed or hypervelocity impacts. They explored the behavior of materials like glass-polycarbonate, ultra-high strength concrete, and reinforced concrete under impact loading or nearby explosions. The effects of high-speed projectile impact on polymer/metal composite structures and metallic materials were also analyzed. The role of bonded interfaces in damage progression and the buffering performance of honeycomb composite structures were discussed. The participants also investigated the scattering of fragments caused by flying objects and the destruction of window glass in buildings.

Hydrogen/Oxygen Detonation and Ignition Characteristics (52 items，4.9%) slope=0.11%/yr

The participants discussed a wide range of topics related to detonation and ignition characteristics, particularly focusing on hydrogen/air and multi-component fuel blends. They explored the effects of various factors on detonation transition, flame acceleration, and ignition delay time. The discussions also delved into the impact of small disturbances, mixture ratios, and reactivity on detonation structures and propagation behavior. Additionally, the use of numerical analysis and modeling in understanding these phenomena was a recurring theme. Safety assessments based on detonation characteristics and the observation of cool flame in high-pressure ignition were also highlighted.

Detonation Transition and Propagation Studies (41 items，3.9%) slope=0.24%/yr

The participants discussed various aspects of deflagration-to-detonation transition (DDT) and detonation propagation. They explored the effects of flame shape differences, suppression methods using sintered wire mesh and porous walls, and the impact of sudden expansion. They also examined the role of obstacles and T-junctions in flame acceleration and detonation initiation. Visualization techniques were used to study detonation initiation and transition processes. The influence of channel width, tube wall temperature changes, and fuel injection row penetration were also discussed. The use of a detonation tube for combustion wave propagation studies was highlighted, along with the analysis of detonation waves in different combustor setups.

Quantitative Flow Visualization and Measurement Techniques (36 items，3.4%) slope=0.23%/yr

The participants focused on the quantitative measurement and visualization of supersonic flows around various objects, using techniques such as Femtosecond Laser Electronic Excitation Tagging (FLEET) and the double-pass BOS method. They also discussed the measurement of drag coefficients of spherical projectiles and the impact of imaging conditions on these measurements. The discussion included the evaluation of atmospheric disturbances and turbulence, as well as the use of molecular imaging technology for pressure distribution measurement on free-flying objects. The applicability of these methods in different scenarios, such as high-speed projectiles and reentry capsule models, was also explored.

Background-Oriented Schlieren (BOS) Techniques in Flow Visualization (35 items，3.3%) slope=0.35%/yr

The participants focused on improving the resolution and accuracy of the Background-Oriented Schlieren (BOS) method for flow field visualization. They discussed the use of deep learning, adaptive optics, and neural networks for super-resolution techniques. The influence of background image tilt and vibration environments on BOS was also considered. The development of new methods, such as the Calibrated Focusing Schlieren and color separation color schlieren, were proposed for quantitative visualization. The transition from laser interferometry CT to BOS-CT was also mentioned. The potential of BOS in various applications, such as shock wave measurement and plane impinging jets visualization, was highlighted.

Plasma Flow and High-Temperature Material Testing (35 items，3.3%) slope=-0.36%/yr

The participants focused on the study of high-temperature plasma flows, particularly those involving silicon-based and carbon-based materials. They discussed various methods for measuring temperature, electron density, and enthalpy in these flows, including spectroscopic techniques, laser Thomson scattering, and the use of cavity probes and electromagnetic force models. They also explored the use of arc-heated wind tunnels, explosive welding, and nanosecond pulse plasma actuators. The potential for in-situ observation and three-dimensional temperature distribution measurement was also highlighted.

The group also touched on the use of automatic coating devices, the impact of preparation methods on PSP performance, and the application of PSP in microduct gas flow measurements.

The role of gas species and ionization promoters in MHD shock layer expansion was also explored. Additionally, the potential of electromagnetic force in enhancing impact force for enthalpy measurement was discussed.
